# Campaign Structure and Narrative Design

The campaign architecture is built around a standardized 28-day progression arc, recently expanded and refined through high-fidelity story implementations like *Static Between Stations*. The system balances mechanical progression with narrative escalation across four distinct phases.

## Core Campaign Framework
The high-level progression is detailed in **context.md**, focusing on phase-based evolution and weekly thematic shifts.

### The 28-Day Standard Arc
As documented in **campaign_structure.md**, the campaign follows a four-week lifecycle:
*   **Week 1 (Intake/Baseline):** Establishing evidence baselines and verifying initial contact.
*   **Week 2 (Contradictions):** Mapping inconsistencies and pressure testing witness accounts.
*   **Week 3 (Escalation):** Transitioning to live interventions and direct antagonist contact.
*   **Week 4 (Endgame):** Resolving final narrative branches and closing the evidence loop.

## Advanced Campaign Implementation
The **static_between_stations_story.md** entry details the *director-cut-v3-hand-authored* upgrade, which extends the operational window to a 45-day structured plan while maintaining the 4-week core structure.

### Architectural Decisions & Flow
*   **Narrative Flow:** Breach & Validation → Interference & Exposure → Active Threat → Ultimatum & Judgement.
*   **Thematic Focus:** Centers on the tension between reality and forgery, specifically targeting institutional concealment.
*   **Operational Rules:** Success is governed by three critical criteria:
    1.  Timeline construction with explicit source confidence levels.
    2.  Cross-checked witness testimony.
    3.  Rigorous evidence chain integrity.
